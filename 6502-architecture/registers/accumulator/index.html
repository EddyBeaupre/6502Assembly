Accumulator, 6502 Accumulator

Description of 6502 Accumulators

The 6502 family microprocessors usually consists of six registers. Accumulators, being one of the six registers, are always regarded as the most important registers . It is because the 6502 family microprocessors regards their accumulators as a place storing the result from almost all types of arithmetic and logical operations.


The relationship between Accumulator and Instruction Set

Accumulators have been involved in most of the operations in the instruction set.

The value stored in an accumulator can be incremented (INC) or decremented (DEC). And they are connected directly to data bus, thus, their value can be loaded (LDA), saved in (STA) or compared with (CMP) the values stored in memory.

The accumulator is responsible for arithmetic operations such as: ADC and SBC. And it is the register that capable for bit manipulation operations, such as: LSR, ROL, ROR
