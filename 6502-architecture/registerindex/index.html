Register:Index

There are two Index Registers for a normal 6502 family microprocessor, namely Register X and Register Y. Both Register X and Register Y are 8 bit. They are normally acted as counters or storing offsets for accessing memory. Their values can be incremented (INX, INY) or decremented (DEX, DEY). And they are connected directly to data bus, thus, their value can be loaded (LDX, LDY ) , saved in (STX, STY) or compared with (CPX , CPY) the values stored in memory.
