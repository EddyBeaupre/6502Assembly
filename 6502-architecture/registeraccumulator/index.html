Purpose of the Register Accumulator

Accumulators are one of the most important registers found in CPUs or microprocessors.

One of the purpose of Register Accumulator is to serve as a place for arithmetic calculation. It is because most of the microprocessors including the 6502 family microprocessors regards their accumulators as a place storing the result from almost all types of arithmetic and logical operations. Accumulators have been involved in a lot of operations mentioned in the instruction set.

The value stored in an accumulator can be incremented (INC) or decremented (DEC). And they are connected directly to data bus, thus, their value can be loaded (LDA), saved in (STA) or compared with (CMP) the values stored in memory.

The accumulator is responsible for arithmetic operations such as: ADC and SBC. And it is the register that capable for bit manipulation operations, such as: LSR, ROL, ROR .
